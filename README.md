# NETFLIX-CONTENT-STRATEGY-ANALYSIS

About Netflix

Netflix is a leading global subscription-based streaming platform. Originally founded in 1997 as a DVD rental service, it has evolved into a major producer of original content, including hits like Stranger Things and Money Heist.

Problem Statement

The streaming industry is highly competitive, with Netflix facing strong pressure from rivals like Amazon Prime Video and Disney+. Key challenges include: 

Subscriber Dynamics: Balancing new user acquisition with existing subscriber retention.  

Rising Costs: High investments required for quality original content. 

Market Trends: Increasing demand for regional content and rapid changes in viewer preferences. 

Operational Issues: Addressing password sharing and ensuring data privacy.  

Dataset Overview

The project utilizes a Netflix dataset sourced from Kaggle. 

https://d2beiqkhq929f0.cloudfront.net/public_assets/assets/000/000/940/original/netflix.csv

Content: Detailed information on movies and TV shows, including Show ID, Type, Title, Director, Cast, Country, Release Year, and Genre.  

Purpose: To analyze content distribution, growth trends, and country-wise production. 

Tools Used

Analysis & Cleaning: Google Colab / Jupyter Notebook (Python). 

Visualization: Tableau. 

https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/dhana.laxmi.kinthali/viz/book10_17730574496310/Dashboard1?publish=yes

Data Preparation & Analysis

Cleaning Steps

Converted Date added to standardized Date formats to extract "Year Added" and "Month Added".  

Split the Duration column into separate numerical fields for Movie minutes and TV Show seasons. 

Handled multi-valued columns like Country, Genre, and Cast that were separated by commas. 

Python Analysis

The following exploratory data analysis (EDA) steps were performed:

Basic Metrics: Identifying dataset shape, info, and summary statistics. 

Detection: Locating missing values and outliers.  

Analysis Types: Univariate and bivariate analysis using plots such as Displots, Countplots, and Heatmaps.  

Key Insights

Content Growth: Aggressive expansion occurred after 2015, with peak growth between 2018 and 2020. 

Global Production: While the United States leads in production, India and Japan are also major contributors. 

Genre Trends: Drama, Comedy, and International Movies are the most common genres. 

Recommendations

Series Content: Continue investing in TV Shows and original series as they drive longer viewer engagement.

Localized Strategy: Increase production in high-growth markets like India and South Korea to reach wider audiences.  

Variety: Maintain a balanced mix of genres, focusing on trending categories like Documentaries and Thrillers.  

Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates Netflix's rapid library expansion and diversification. By continuing to focus on regional markets and trending genres, Netflix can strengthen its global leadership.
